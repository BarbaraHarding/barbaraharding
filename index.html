My IT Project

My passion has just been validated completely by the personality and learning tests that I have just completed, and in light of that my ultimate IT project would be to create a program or way of collecting and collating data to analyse, define and assess risk factors contributing to domestic and personal violence in our communities, and use this to assist health and community services in appropriation of resources within our community to better mitigate risks to safety of workers in the field and the safety of at-risk members of the public.

This project is important because so much attention is focussed on personal safety in our communities. There is a direct correlation between environment and the risk of personal safety incidence and with the sheer volume of data available from so many public sources there must be a way of collecting and filtering this data to better analyse and predict risk factors without infringing on individual privacy.

This project centres around collection of publicly available social data and using this data to gain a clearer understanding of the source of risk to health of members of our community who most need outside help to mitigate that risk. There is so much social data available today that could be used to identify persons who are at risk, or maybe more clearly define the risk surrounding a particular individual so that we, as a community can better protect them. The aim is to use data collected to pinpoint high risk triggers for violence and other safety concerns within our communities so that we can assess these and as a community provide better protection for at-risk members of the public and health and service workers.

The project would benefit the whole of our community by improving the safety of our most at-risk community members such as children, people with disabilities, victims of domestic violence, members of marginalised social groups.

There are many dangers in attempting a project such as this, and privacy is just one of them. Collecting large amounts of data about people is dangerous, especially data that can be used to identify individuals. Privacy laws are in place to protect everyone and must be respected. Some of the obstacles include; who has access to the data and who makes a judgement call in any given case. What parameters are set around data collection and who sets the parameters. How is the data stored and for how long. Which services would have access to information on individuals and how much access does each service get? And then, who makes that decision? Even if the project only uses publicly available data there is still so very much room for risk in collection and use that the set up would be vitally important.

As a novice in the IT field, I think the tools and technologies that are required for this project are still unknown to me. It is completely over-ambitious at this stage for me to try and list actual available tools, as I am not sure I am able to explain well enough for my own comfort the tools I am being exposed to within the course so far, let alone others that I am required to find and define for this project. Suffice to say the project requires a data collection technique, a collaborative platform for data management, access for a team to manage and analyse the collected data. And a set of publicly trusted overseers to manage the rules of the project and keep it accountable.

The skills required for this project include a legal understanding around the privacy act, data collection and use; Data collection skills; Community health experience; Data analysis.

Software for data collection and categorisation and security will be required. Public health and emergency services experts are required for consultation.

When this project is successful, the outcome will be that social and emergency services will have a clearer set of parameters to work within when allocating resources in communities. There will be clearer guidelines for assessing risks in individual cases leading to fewer cases falling through the cracks and overall improved community wellbeing.
